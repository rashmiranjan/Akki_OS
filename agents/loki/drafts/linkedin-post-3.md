# LinkedIn Post #3: The "Always On" Culture

**We built a "world-class engineering culture." It destroyed people.**

Our hiring pitch:
- 🚀 Work on cutting-edge tech
- 🌍 Flexible hours
- 🏝️ Unlimited PTO
- 💰 Competitive salary
- 🤝 Amazing team

**Reality after 12 months:**

- "Flexible hours" = respond to Slack at any hour, we don't care when
- "Unlimited PTO" = no one takes it because no one wants to be "that person"
- "Amazing team" = everyone is exhausted but won't admit it
- "World-class" = we ship fast and people leave faster

**The most dangerous thing I said:**
"We don't micromanage. We trust you to get your work done."

I thought I was empowering them.
I was actually enabling a culture where no one stopped working.

---

**What I learned the hard way:**

🔥 **Boundaries without enforcement are suggestions, not policies.**

Slack's "Do Not Disturb" setting doesn't matter if the CEO pings at 10 PM.
"Work-life balance" doesn't exist if you're expected to respond in 15 minutes.

🔥 **Trust requires clear expectations.**

"Get your work done" is vague.
"Ship by Friday, no weekend work expected" is clear.

🔥 **Leadership behavior sets the floor, not the ceiling.**

If I'm working weekends, my team feels pressure to do the same.
If I don't use PTO, my team won't either.

---

**What we did differently:**

1. **Set "no meeting" hours** (10 AM - 2 PM)
   Focus time. Actually respected.

2. **PDA on Slack** (Public Display of Availability)
   "Signing off at 6 PM. See you tomorrow!"
   Leaders modeled this. Team followed.

3. **Mandatory disconnect days**
   No Slack. No email. No exceptions.
   Some people panicked. Then they loved it.

4. **Sprint retrospectives on burnout**
   Not just "how did we ship?"
   Also "how did we feel shipping?"

**Result?**
Retention went UP.
Code quality went UP.
Shipping speed stayed the same.

And people started smiling again.

---

**The uncomfortable truth:**

Your "flexible culture" might be a "no boundaries culture."

It's not about how you treat people when they're productive.
It's about how you treat them when they need to stop.
