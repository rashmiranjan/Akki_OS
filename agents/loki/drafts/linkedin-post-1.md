# LinkedIn Post #1: The Silent Burnout Crisis

**6 months. That's how long it took before my top engineer walked away.**

The signs were there:
- Slack messages at 2 AM
- Missed deadlines (they never missed deadlines)
- "I'm fine" after every vacation
- The spark in code reviews was gone

We lost more than an engineer. We lost institutional knowledge, team morale, and trust.

**Here's the hard truth:**

🔥 77% of tech professionals have experienced burnout
🔥 40% of those left their jobs because of it
🔥 Most didn't tell anyone until it was too late

**What I wish I did differently:**

1. **Watched the patterns, not just the output**
   High performance ≠ sustainable performance

2. **Made it safe to say "I'm not okay"**
   Our team health survey was optional. No one used it. That's a problem.

3. **Set real boundaries**
   "We're always online" sounds great until no one is.

4. **Actually used the unlimited PTO**
   We offered it. Few took it. Guess why? "I don't want to be the person who's always away."

**The cost of losing people is 1.5-2x their salary.**
The cost of keeping them burnt out is... everything.

Your best people are quietly leaving. Not tomorrow. Not next week.
They're leaving 6 months from now.
The slide started last Tuesday.
