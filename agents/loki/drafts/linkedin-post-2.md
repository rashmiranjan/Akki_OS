# LinkedIn Post #2: The "High Performer" Trap

**I almost lost my best engineer. Here's why I didn't see it coming.**

They were crushing it.
- Shipped features on time
- Never said no
- Always available on Slack
- Took the toughest tickets

**I thought: This is what rockstars look like.**

I was wrong. This is what burnout looks like.

---

**The High Performer's Dilemma:**

The people who care the most are the ones who burn out first.

They don't say "I'm overwhelmed."
They say "I've got this."

They don't say "I need help."
They say "I'll just work through the weekend."

They don't say "I'm spiraling."
They say "I'm fine."

**And here's the dangerous part:**
Managers reward this behavior.
"Hero awards" for late nights.
Recognition for sacrificing weekends.
Promotions for "getting it done."

We're literally incentivizing the behavior that destroys them.

---

**What changed for us:**

I stopped looking at output. I started looking at patterns.

- Is this sustainable? (Not just today, but 6 months from now?)
- Are they learning or just grinding?
- Do they have time to think, or just time to execute?

**The day I told my top engineer:**
"No more late nights. If we can't ship this within work hours, we're scoping it wrong."

They didn't quit.
They slept better.
Their code got cleaner.
And the team? They started trusting me.

---

**Here's the question:**
Are you building a team of heroes or a team of humans?

Heroes make great stories. Humans make great products.

I chose humans. Best decision I ever made.
